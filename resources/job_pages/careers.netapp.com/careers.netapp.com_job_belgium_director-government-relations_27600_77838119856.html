Job Summary

NetApp is the expert in Intelligent Data Infrastructure, offering intelligent data management, storage and security solutions to customers around the world. NetApp is headquartered in Silicon Valley and Cork, Ireland, with other offices throughout the Americas, Asia and Europe. NetApp has a very strong public sector business, especially in the United States where our federal government market share is number one.

We are seeking a Director of EMEA Government Relations to lead our government relations activity within Europe, the Middle East, and Africa. Under the direction of the Vice President of Global Government Relations, the successful candidate will develop and implement strategies to advocate for favorable policies, ensure regulatory compliance and maintain the company’s positive image across diverse political landscapes in support of revenue generation for Public Sector sales in EMEA. This high-stakes role is ideal for a diplomatic, strategic thinker with deep knowledge of the EMEA region’s political and regulatory frameworks. The GR function is part of the NetApp legal department, and this position reports to NetApp’s Vice President of Global Government Relations.

We offer a dynamic, collaborative and innovative work environment that has made the legal department recognized and awarded by several US and International organizations, and the newly formed government relations function aspires to the same level of excellence. If you love adding value, making a difference and having a corporate culture where it’s all about the people and results, NetApp is for you!

Key Responsibilities

  • Government Relations Strategy: Develop and execute comprehensive government relations strategies that support the company's business objectives in the EMEA region.
  • Policy Analysis and Advocacy: Monitor and analyze regulatory proposals and political trends, advising company leadership on potential impacts. Advocate for policy positions beneficial to the organization and its stakeholders.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Establish and nurture relationships with government officials, regulatory agencies, trade associations, and other key influencers in the EMEA political environment.
  • Issue Management: Proactively identify issues that could impact the company and lead the response strategy to minimize risks. Coordinate with legal and compliance teams to ensure adherence to all applicable laws and regulations.
  • Cross-Functional Collaboration: Work collaboratively with internal teams such as Public Affairs, Legal, Compliance, and Business Units to integrate government relations activities with broader company strategies.
  • Influencer Network Development: Utilize and further build his/her existing network with other technology firms, non-governmental organizations, and thought leaders to shape public policy debates in line with the company's interests.
  • Communications: Craft messaging and position statements on policy issues, preparing briefing materials for executive leadership and representing the company at public events as required.
  • Crisis Management: Provide counsel on governmental matters during any reputational or regulatory crisis, ensuring swift and appropriate responses.

Job Requirements

  • Experience: Minimum of 10-15 years of related experience in government relations or public affairs, with a proven track record of success as a senior government relations professional in the EMEA region. Experience in the technology sector is highly regarded.
  • Knowledge: Comprehensive understanding of EMEA governmental processes, regulatory environments, and geopolitical factors influencing public policy.
  • Language Skills: Fluency in English; additional language proficiency in one or more other languages in the EMEA region is highly desirable.
  • Communication: Exceptional verbal and written communication skills, with the ability to simplify complex regulatory language and communicate convincingly to a variety of audiences; experience in public speaker in front of large audiences, senior executives and government officials.

Data privacy

We care about your privacy and therefore ask that you read our Applicant Privacy Policy (PDF) before you submit any personal information to us.

NetApp may use an automated employment evaluation tool or similar tool as one of several tools, actions, and/or steps to assist with NetApp’s review of candidate applications for various hiring needs. Currently, when addressing certain hiring needs, NetApp uses the Eightfold tool which can provide an initial ranking of a candidate’s skills and experience, based on information provided by the applicant in the application and/or supporting documentation, in comparison to the NetApp designated key requirements of a specific role. Additionally, the tool may be used to help review and /or rank internal employees seeking promotion or other internal mobility.

An independent audit of the Eightfold Matching Model tool can be found at https://eightfold.ai/nyc-eightfoldmatching-model.

Candidates may request an alternative selection process which will not be subject to the Eightfold matching tool or to any electronic automated employment evaluation by contacting NetApp at careers@netapp.com. To bypass the Eightfold matching tool or any electronic automated employment evaluation, you must include a resume and job ID with your email to careers@netapp.com and you must include in the subject line of your email: Data Privacy Request. Candidates who have questions or want to request additional information on the source of data, type of data, and/or collection of data related to the candidate review process should contact NetApp at careers@netapp.com
