Who You’ll Work With
You’ll collaborate with production engineering teams, quality assurance personnel, and logistics staff to ensure efficient manufacturing and delivery of high-quality products. You’ll also engage with other assembly technicians and cross-functional teams to troubleshoot issues and enhance processes.
What You’ll Do 
The tasks involved in the manufacturing process include assembling product subassemblies and final assemblies with precision and care, testing products and subassemblies to ensure functionality and adherence to quality standards, and inspecting finished products for quality and compliance with customer specifications. Additionally, troubleshooting equipment, devices, or product-related issues and providing feedback to production engineering are crucial responsibilities. Operators are required to handle production equipment in line with good manufacturing practices and standard operating procedures, maintain accurate records in webMES for timely and efficient production tracking, and clean, prepare, and package materials for customer shipments. Adhering to and promoting safety protocols on the manufacturing floor is also a critical aspect of the role. Furthermore, employees are occasionally exposed to moving mechanical parts and fumes or airborne particles, and the noise level in the work environment is usually moderate to high due to the nature of the product and equipment.
Who You Are
Diligent with strong manual dexterity and critical thinking skills, the individual is proficient in computer use and comfortable working with sensitive equipment. They are skilled at reading and following written instructions, capable of maintaining focus and precision in a dynamic, fast-paced environment, and demonstrate adaptability, collaboration, and a commitment to continuous improvement.
Minimum Qualifications
• Possession of a High School diploma or equivalent
• Ability to stand for the duration of a working shift and lift or move objects weighing up to 50 pounds.
• Demonstrated basic computer proficiency, the ability to operate specialized equipment and familiarity with manufacturing processes. 
• Familiarity with safety protocols and vision abilities, including close vision, peripheral vision, depth perception, and the ability to adjust focus.
• Availability to work flexible schedules, including evenings and weekends, to meet business needs.
Preferred Qualifications
• Experience in semiconductor or medical-related manufacturing.
• Knowledge of lean manufacturing principles.
• Familiarity with microscopes, selective attention, and deductive reasoning skills.
• Typing proficiency and experience with webMES systems.
• Previous experience troubleshooting and optimizing production equipment.

U.S. employees have access to quality medical, dental and vision insurance, a 401(k) plan with a Cisco matching contribution, short and long-term disability coverage, basic life insurance and numerous wellbeing offerings.

Employees receive up to twelve paid holidays per calendar year, which includes one floating holiday (for non-exempt employees), plus a day off for their birthday. Non-Exempt new hires accrue up to 16 days of vacation time off each year, at a rate of 4.92 hours per pay period. Exempt new hires participate in Cisco’s flexible Vacation Time Off policy, which does not place a defined limit on how much vacation time eligible employees may use, but is subject to availability and some business limitations. All new hires are eligible for Sick Time Off subject to Cisco’s Sick Time Off Policy and will have eighty (80) hours of sick time off provided on their hire date and on January 1st of each year thereafter.  Up to 80 hours of unused sick time will be carried forward from one calendar year to the next such that the maximum number of sick time hours an employee may have available is 160 hours. We offer additional paid time to volunteer and give back to the community.

Employees on sales plans earn performance-based incentive pay on top of their base salary, which is split between quota and non-quota components. For quota-based incentive pay, Cisco typically pays as follows:

.75% of incentive target for each 1% of revenue attainment up to 50% of quota;

1.5% of incentive target for each 1% of attainment between 50% and 75%;

1% of incentive target for each 1% of attainment between 75% and 100%; and once performance exceeds 100% attainment, incentive rates are at or above 1% for each 1% of attainment with no cap on incentive compensation.

For non-quota-based sales performance elements such as strategic sales objectives, Cisco may pay up to 125% of target. Cisco sales plans do not have a minimum threshold of performance for sales incentive compensation to be paid.
